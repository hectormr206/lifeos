# Gemini Prompt — Official LifeOS Wallpaper

Prompt maestro para generar con Gemini el wallpaper canonico de LifeOS.

Objetivo:

- producir una primera version del wallpaper oficial
- evaluar si la direccion visual de `Axi Xochimilco` funciona
- usarlo como base para iterar, no como salida final automatica sin revision

---

## Prompt Principal

```text
Create a premium 4K wallpaper for LifeOS, an AI-native Linux operating system.

The wallpaper must establish the official visual identity of LifeOS.
Its central symbol is Axi, a Mexican axolotl, and the image should make the axolotl feel iconic in the same way that other platforms have a single instantly recognizable symbol.

Art direction:
- elegant
- atmospheric
- dark-first
- premium
- calm
- technical
- memorable
- not childish
- not cartoonish
- not touristy

Core concept:
This should be the canonical LifeOS wallpaper, called "Axi Xochimilco".
It should depict a recognizable axolotl in a dark, aquatic, nocturnal atmosphere inspired by Xochimilco and the Mexican origin of the brand.

The wallpaper must feel like:
- sovereign computing
- local-first intelligence
- privacy by default
- Mexican identity handled with subtlety and dignity
- a living system with memory and presence

Composition requirements:
- 16:9 wallpaper composition
- 4K resolution feel
- one clear focal subject: the axolotl
- the axolotl should be elegant, slightly mysterious, and serene
- the background should suggest dark water, reflections, aquatic vegetation, soft mist, or bioluminescent particles
- the image should have enough negative space so desktop icons remain readable
- no text
- no logos
- no UI overlays

LifeOS brand palette:
- Axi Teal: #00D4AA
- Axi Pink: #FF6B9D
- Deep Night: #0F0F1B
- Midnight Surface: #161830
- Soft White: #E8E8E8

Color behavior:
- teal is the primary accent
- pink is secondary and subtle, mainly for gills or small highlights
- the overall image should remain dark and restrained
- avoid bright daylight
- avoid oversaturated rainbow colors

Stylistic constraints:
- do not make it look like a children’s illustration
- do not make it look like a generic fantasy creature
- do not use a cliche cyberpunk purple aesthetic
- do not make it look like anime fan art
- do not make it look like a tourism poster
- do not dress the axolotl in literal folk costume
- do not turn it into a mascot sticker

Desired visual language:
- premium concept-art quality
- subtle cinematic lighting
- soft volumetric atmosphere
- refined edges and forms
- a balance between realism and stylization
- enough character to feel iconic, but restrained enough to live as a daily wallpaper

Brand-specific symbolism:
- the axolotl should feel like the soul of the operating system
- resilient, observant, local, intelligent, alive
- the image should suggest that LifeOS is rooted in Mexico but made for a global technological future

Output goal:
Generate the most convincing official wallpaper for LifeOS.
It should feel like a wallpaper people would remember and immediately associate with the project.
```

---

## Negative Prompt

```text
cartoon, childish mascot, chibi, tourist poster, sombrero cliché, mariachi costume, kitsch folklore, generic neon cyberpunk, purple overload, anime, cute sticker, mobile game art, flat vector, clipart, low detail, busy background, hard text overlays, logo overlays, unrealistic rainbow palette, comic style, exaggerated facial expression, comedy tone
```

---

## Variante Mas Minimal

Usa esta variacion si quieres una version mas sobria:

```text
Create a more minimal and premium variant of the LifeOS official wallpaper.
Keep the same axolotl identity and Xochimilco-inspired atmosphere, but reduce visual clutter.
Use softer detail, stronger silhouette, more negative space, and a calmer composition suitable for daily desktop use.
The axolotl should remain clearly recognizable, but the scene should feel more meditative than illustrative.
```

## Checklist de Evaluacion

- ¿Se reconoce claramente como ajolote?
- ¿Se siente LifeOS y no una ilustracion generica?
- ¿Se ve premium y usable como wallpaper diario?
- ¿Respeta la paleta teal/rosa/dark-first?
- ¿Se siente mexicano sin caer en cliché?
- ¿Tiene suficiente identidad para ser canonico?
